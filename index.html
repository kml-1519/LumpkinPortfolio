Socio-Demographic Data Dashboards

The beta version of the Socio-Demographic Data Dashboards provides interactive visualizations of local demographic, environmental, and housing conditions for select Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) Fiscal Year 2023 jurisdictions. These comprehensive dashboards integrate interactive maps, analytical text, data tables, and dynamic charts using multiple authoritative data sources, including the 2017-2021 5-Year American Community Survey, Department of Transportation metrics, Environmental Protection Agency indicators, and HUD administrative datasets. These dashboards were designed to enable local governments, Public Housing Agencies, states, and community organizations to conduct evidence-based housing needs assessments, streamline preparation of HUD-mandated reports, and develop targeted housing and community development strategies.

Site Suitability: Siting Climate Resilient Housing in the U.S. Virgin Islands

I conducted a comprehensive site suitability analysis using ArcGIS Pro to identify optimal locations for climate-resilient affordable housing on St. Thomas in the U.S. Virgin Islands. This multi-criteria evaluation incorporated eight critical factors: appropriate slope gradients (prioritizing areas with 0-10% slope), zoning compliance (focusing on R-3, R-4, R-5, and B-4 zones), proximity to public transportation (within 305 feet of bus stops), access to commercial centers (within ¼ mile), and safety from four distinct climate hazards (gut flooding, sea level rise, tsunami inundation, and storm surge).

Using weighted overlay techniques and the ArcGIS Pro Suitability Modeler, I transformed complex environmental and social data into actionable development recommendations. The analysis revealed the Anna's Retreat estate as the most suitable area for multi-family housing development—balancing climate resilience with accessibility to essential services. I completed this analysis for the University of Pennsylvania's City Planning Studio on St. Thomas's Resilience Plan.

Technical Assistance Funding Dashboard

I designed a custom dashboard for senior leadership in the Office of Fair Housing and Equal Opportunity (FHEO) to strategically monitor technical assistance (TA) funding allocations. Leveraging Power Automate for automated data integration and Power BI for analytics, the dashboard transformed financial data into intuitive visual metrics that highlighted opportunities for optimization. This interactive dashboard enabled leadership to make evidence-based decisions about resource allocation, prioritize technical assistance interventions, and maximize the impact of limited funding. The dashboard's real-time monitoring capabilities significantly enhanced FHEO's ability to respond dynamically to changing priorities while maintaining compliance with federal requirements.

Affirmatively Furthering Fair Housing and Environmental Justice

I co-authored a white paper for the Department of Housing and Urban Development's Office of Fair Housing and Equal Opportunity Policy and Legislative Initiatives Division that established the critical intersection between environmental justice and fair housing policy. My analysis provided senior leadership with evidence-based recommendations for integrating environmental justice principles into the Affirmatively Furthering Fair Housing (AFFH) Rule, a key regulatory framework requiring jurisdictions to address discriminatory housing patterns. The white paper specifically demonstrated how environmental hazard exposure disproportionately affects protected classes, and how AFFH implementation could be strengthened through systematic assessment of these disparities. This work directly informed the Department's approach to compliance reviews and technical assistance priorities, helping jurisdictions develop more effective fair housing strategies.

Preserving Generational Wealth for Heirs Property Homeowners

I authored a comprehensive policy brief on heirs property - a fractional ownership crisis affecting an estimated 440,000 properties valued at $41 billion nationwide. This research identified how the absence of estate planning creates an unstable form of ownership that disproportionately undermines wealth-building for Black, Latino, Hispanic, and low-income households. While at the PolicyCenter, I developed a multi-pronged policy framework addressing both prevention and resolution strategies, including expanding affordable legal services, creating alternative pathways to verify ownership, and strengthening federal programs like the Heirs Property Relending Program. The brief has served as an actionable roadmap for policymakers, financial institutions, and housing advocates working to preserve generational wealth and housing stability for vulnerable communities facing this complex legal challenge.

Land Use Regulation in the City of Philadelphia

This paper, written for a University of Pennsylvania Land Use Law course, analyzes Philadelphia's zoning appeal process through two contrasting Zoning Board of Adjustment (ZBA) case studies. I examine how the city's ZBA navigates community input when considering variances, revealing tensions between theory and practice in urban land regulation. My analysis shows that appointed ZBA members often favor development interests over community and planning commission recommendations, with limited transparency in their decision-making. The paper demonstrates how procedural limitations in Philadelphia's land use system can perpetuate inequalities, particularly in historically marginalized neighborhoods like Tioga and Point Breeze, raising important questions about the democratic principles underlying municipal zoning practices.
